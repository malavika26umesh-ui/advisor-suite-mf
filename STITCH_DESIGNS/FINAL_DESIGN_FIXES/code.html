Total Expense Ratio (TER): What It Is and How It Affects You

calendar_today 14 Jan 2026
link SEBI Circular Reference 2025/12
schedule 8 min read

What is TER?

The Total Expense Ratio (TER) represents the operational cost of managing a mutual fund scheme. For retail investors in India, understanding this metric is crucial as it directly impacts the net take-home returns. It covers everything from the fund manager's salary to administrative overheads.

"TER is the sum of all expenses incurred by a mutual fund scheme for its management, operation, and marketing, divided by the fund's total assets."

How is TER Calculated?

Mutual funds calculate their expenses daily. This amount is subtracted from the Net Asset Value (NAV) of the fund before the closing NAV is announced to the public. This means the returns you see on your dashboard are already net of these expenses.

  • Total Scheme Assets: ₹1,000 Crores
  • Total Annual Expenses: ₹15 Crores
  • TER = (15 / 1,000) * 100 = 1.50%

SEBI's Maximum TER Caps

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) mandates strict limits on how much an AMC can charge based on the scheme's Assets Under Management (AUM). These slabs ensure economies of scale are passed to the investors.

AUM Slab (Equity Funds) Maximum Permissible TER
First ₹500 Crores 2.25%
Next ₹250 Crores 2.00%
Next ₹1,250 Crores 1.75%
Next ₹3,000 Crores 1.60%
