Understanding Exit Load

Last checked: 18 Jun 2026 · Version: v4

A comprehensive guide to how exit loads impact your mutual fund returns and why they exist in the regulatory framework.

1

Definition of Exit Load

Exit load is a fee charged by Asset Management Companies (AMCs) when an investor sells or redeems their mutual fund units within a specific pre-defined period from the date of investment.

2

The "Discouragement" Logic

The primary objective is to discourage premature withdrawals. By penalizing early exits, AMCs aim to ensure stability in the fund's corpus and protect long-term investors from the costs of frequent liquidity management.

3

Calculation Methodology

It is typically expressed as a percentage of the Net Asset Value (NAV). For example, if the NAV is ₹100 and the exit load is 1%, ₹1 will be deducted, and the investor will receive ₹99 per unit.

4

Holding Period Requirements

Different funds have different windows. Equity funds often have a 1-year exit load window, while liquid funds might have a sliding scale load that disappears after just 7 days.

5

FIFO (First-In, First-Out) Rule

When you redeem partially, AMCs apply the FIFO principle. The units bought first are considered sold first, which helps in determining if those specific units have crossed the exit-load-free threshold.

6

Taxation Interaction

The exit load paid is not tax-deductible. It is treated as a reduction in the sale consideration, effectively reducing your capital gains (or increasing your loss) for tax calculation purposes.
