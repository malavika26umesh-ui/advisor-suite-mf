Are there any exit loads on Liquid Funds?

Asked by Retail Investor • 2 hours ago

verified

Yes, SEBI introduced a graded exit load structure for liquid funds in 2019. If you withdraw your investment within 7 days of allotment, a small exit load is applicable, starting at 0.0070% on Day 1 and reducing to 0% after Day 6.

This move was implemented to prevent institutional investors from using liquid funds for ultra-short overnight parking, which could cause volatility. From the 7th day onwards, there is zero exit load on liquid fund redemptions.

What is Exit Load?

An exit load is a fee charged by the Asset Management Company (AMC) when an investor sells or redeems their mutual fund units within a specific timeframe.

Key Purpose: To discourage short-term withdrawals and protect the interests of long-term investors in the fund.
