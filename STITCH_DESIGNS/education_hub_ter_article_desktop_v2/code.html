Total Expense Ratio (TER): What It Is and How It Affects You

calendar_today 14 Jan 2026
link SEBI Circular Reference 2025/12 open_in_new
schedule 8 min read

What is TER?

The Total Expense Ratio (TER) represents the annual operating costs of a mutual fund scheme, expressed as a percentage of the fund's daily net assets. It is a critical metric for investors as it directly reduces the net returns generated by the scheme.

info

Definition

TER is the sum of all expenses incurred by a mutual fund, including management fees, administrative costs, audit fees, and marketing expenses, divided by the fund's total assets.

Lower TERs generally translate to higher take-home returns for the investor over the long term. In India, SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) strictly regulates the maximum TER a fund can charge to protect investor interests.

How is TER Calculated?

The calculation is straightforward but the components behind it are diverse. All expenses are accounted for on a daily basis and reflected in the fund's Net Asset Value (NAV).

calculate Calculation Example

Total Scheme Assets: ₹1,000 Crores

Total Annual Expenses: ₹15 Crores

TER = (15 / 1,000) * 100 = 1.50%

Note: This 1.50% is deducted gradually from the daily NAV, meaning the returns you see in your statement are already net of these costs.

SEBI's Maximum TER Caps

SEBI has established tiered limits based on the Assets Under Management (AUM) of the fund. As a fund grows larger, the allowed TER percentage decreases, passing the benefit of scale to the investors.

AUM Slab (Equity Funds) Maximum Permissible TER
First ₹500 Crores 2.25%
Next ₹250 Crores 2.00%
Next ₹1,250 Crores 1.75%
Next ₹3,000 Crores 1.60%
